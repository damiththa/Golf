How handicap index is calculated

Following along what's descrined here, this is how Handicap index is calculated.

Step 1: In Handicap Formula: Calculate the Differentials

Let say, if your score is 85, course rating is 72.2 and course slope is 131.
Plugging into formula, (Score - Course Rating) x 113 / Slope Rating gives you a Handicap Differential of 11.04.
(Note: 113 is a USGA constant)

Step 2: Determine How Many Differentials To Use

Let say you have 12 rounds under your belt, as the article mentioned you take your 5 lowest differentials.

Step 3: Average Your Differentials

Staying with the same example, 12 rounds, 5 differentials, you find the average (i.e. by dividing the 5 lowest differentials by 5).

Step 4: Arriving At Your Handicap Index

Now take average, let's say it's 11.698, multiply that by 0.96 (another USGA constant) will give you your Handicap Index. 11.698 X 0.96 = 11.23
Therefore your handicap index is 11.2.

As you can see, you need at lease 20 rounds in to get an accurate handicap index.


What this page is doing

My course details, round info. etc. all are recorded in AirTable. Just as I enter my round score, AirTable. automatically calculates and stores my Handicap Differentials (step 1) in tables accordingly.

When this page loads, it makes an API call to AirTable to get all those stored infomation, in accordance to the number of rounds recorded (step 2).

Page then does step 3 and 4 and checks that Handicap Index against what's stored in FireBase as my current handicap.
If different, it updates my current Handicap Index and also makes an entry in the Handicap History node so that chart can display how my Handicap has changed over time.
