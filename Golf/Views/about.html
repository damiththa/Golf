How handicap index is calculated

Following along what's descrined here, this is how Handicap index is calculated.

Step 1 In Handicap Formula: Calculate the Differentials

   (Score - Course Rating) x 113 / Slope Rating
   Let say, if your score is 85, course rating is 72.2 and course slope is 131, plugging into the formula will give you a Handicap Differential of 11.04 (a USGA constant).
(85 - 72.2) x 113 / 131 = 11.04

Step 2: Determine How Many Differentials To Use

   Let say you have 12 rounds under your belt, as the article mentioned you take your 5 lowest differentials.

Step 3: Average Your Differentials

   Staying with the same example, 12 rounds, 5 differentials, you find the average (i.e. dividing the 5 lowest differentials by 5).

Step 4: Arriving At Your Handicap Index

   Now take average (let's say it's 11.698) multiply it by 0.96 (another USGA constant) will give you your Handicap Index. 11.698 X 0.96 = 11.23
Handicap Index 11.2.

As you can see, you need at lease 20 rounds in to get an accurate handicap index.

What this page is doing

All my course details, round info. etc. all are recorded in AirTable. Just as I eneter my round score, it automatically calculates and stores my Handicap Differentials.

When this page is loading, it makes an API call to AirTable to get all stored info according to number of rounds recorded (step 2)

page the does step 3 and 4 and then checks that Handicap Index against what's stored in FireBase as my current handicap.
If different, it updates my current Handicap Index and also makes an entry in the Handicap History node so that chart can display how my Handicap has changed over time.
